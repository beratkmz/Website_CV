Projects

VTOL UAV System for Detecting Collapsed Buildings After Earthquakes

The increasing frequency of disasters worldwide has become a growing concern, with post-earthquake regional assessments playing a critical role in response efforts. As a result, certain government agencies and civil rescue teams have begun utilizing drone systems to support search and rescue operations. However, these drone systems are inefficient compared to VTOL systems and lack specialization for disaster scenarios. Satellite systems are slow and fail to provide "very high" resolution imagery. Moreover, 3D point cloud mapping is significantly more accurate and effective when performed at lower altitudes, closer to the ground.

Our project integrates VTOL and fixed-wing UAVs with advanced AI to deliver rapid, high-resolution mapping, object detection, and situational analysis tailored for emergency response and disaster management. It provides data collection and processing to generate precise, actionable insights for critical sectors such as earthquake response, infrastructure inspection, and environmental monitoring.

Duration: 1 year

Project team size: 6 people + 1 advisor

Project exhibition participated in 18th R&D Project Market - Cankaya University
